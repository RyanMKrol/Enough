## Overview

Build the "Shape your trip" step (Step 2 of 3): trip duration via a native segmented control and
multi-select scenario chips, feeding `OnboardingDraft`, with the accent-colored "Build my plan"
CTA.

## Do

1. Read design-spec.md §2.3 and architecture.md. Scenarios come from
   `services.contentStore.catalog().scenarios` — never hardcoded.
2. Create `Sources/Features/Onboarding/TripShapeView.swift` — `struct TripShapeView: View`,
   reading `\.services`, the shared `OnboardingDraft` (environment) and the flow's navigation
   binding (same mechanism T031/T033 used to push steps):
   - Toolbar: shared `OnboardingBackButton`.
   - Header: `EyebrowLabel("STEP 2 OF 3")`, title "Shape your trip" (30–32 bold, tracking
     −0.02em).
   - Section 1 — question label "How long are you there?" (15 semibold `#6b6b70`), then a
     native `Picker` with `.pickerStyle(.segmented)` and two segments titled exactly
     "A weekend" / "A week", bound to `draft.duration` (`TripDuration.weekend|.week`). Rely on
     the system segmented style (track `#f2f2f7`, white selected segment) — do not rebuild it.
   - Section 2 — question label "What will you be doing?" (same style), then the scenario
     chips: one `ScenarioChip(title:isSelected:action:)` (DesignSystem, T004) per catalog
     scenario, laid out in a wrapping flow. If no flow layout exists yet in the codebase, add
     `Sources/Features/Onboarding/FlowLayout.swift` — a minimal `Layout`-protocol
     implementation (wrap rows, spacing 10) used only here.
     Tapping toggles membership in `draft.scenarioIds` with a spring animation
     (`.spring(response: 0.3, dampingFraction: 0.6)`) and a light impact haptic. Chip visual
     states are the component's own (selected = accent-tinted fill + 1.5pt inset accent ring +
     accent text; unselected = `#f2f2f7` fill, `#6b6b70` text).
   - Pinned bottom: `Button("Build my plan")` with
     `PrimaryButtonStyle(background: draft.accent.accent)` (the country accent chosen in
     Step 1). Enabled regardless of chip count (zero scenarios is allowed — the plan screen
     then seeds from the bundle only); action pushes `.plan`.
   - Background white; horizontal padding ~22; section gap ~24.
3. Replace the `.tripShape` placeholder destination in `OnboardingFlowView` with
   `TripShapeView`.
4. `#Preview` with a draft preseeded to Japan.

## Done when

- Both controls read AND write `OnboardingDraft`: toggling segments/chips, navigating back to
  Step 1 and forward again shows every answer preserved.
- The chips render the six fixture scenarios (Eating out, Getting around, Greetings, Nightlife,
  Shopping, Emergencies) from the catalog, multi-selectable with the specified selected
  treatment.
- "Build my plan" is accent-colored (Japan `#e24947` when Japan is selected) and pushes the
  plan step.
- Verify on the simulator (`./build_run.sh`, navigate Get started → select Japan → Continue):
  screenshot shows eyebrow "STEP 2 OF 3", "Shape your trip", the segmented control with
  "A weekend" selected, the six chips (any selection state), and the red "Build my plan"
  button.
