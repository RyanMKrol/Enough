## Overview

🔒 needs-human. One-time App Store Connect setup so real StoreKit purchases (T062) have products
to talk to. Involves credentials and real-money products — humans only.

## Do

1. In App Store Connect, create the app record for `com.ryankrol.enough` (name "Enough").
2. Create non-consumable IAP products — one per purchasable in `Content/catalog.json`, using the
   catalog ids as product ids (recommended prefix: `com.ryankrol.enough.<catalog-id>`, e.g.
   `com.ryankrol.enough.jp-greetings`, `com.ryankrol.enough.jp-weekend`): 4 Japan decks at
   **£1.00 via App Store flexible pricing (base-price point £1.00, UK storefront)** — NOT the
   old £0.99 tier, so the payment sheet matches the catalog's displayed £1.00 — the Weekend
   bundle (£2.49) and The whole week bundle (£3.49); repeat for France/Germany ids or defer
   those until their content is real.
3. For EACH IAP product, upload the required **review screenshot** and fill the review notes —
   and attach every IAP product to the app's **first version** in App Store Connect (App
   Version page → In-App Purchases section → add them). Both are classic Guideline 2.1
   rejection traps: without them the reviewer cannot see the products at all.
4. Complete the **age-rating questionnaire** (the new 5-tier system, mandatory since
   Jan 31 2026) — Enough is a clean 4+.
5. Record the final product-id ↔ catalog-id mapping convention in
   `.harness/docs/designs/design-spec.md` §0 (append a line) or a short note in this file's
   worklog, so T062 binds to it.
6. Ensure signing/capabilities for in-app purchase are enabled for the bundle id.

## Done when

The product ids exist in App Store Connect (sandbox-testable), the id convention is written
down where T062 can read it, and the owner marks this done via
`.harness/scripts/mark-done.sh T061`.
