## Overview
Lean further into iOS 26 Liquid Glass on the bottom tab bar (Sources/DesignSystem/LiquidGlassTabBar.swift), which today only applies a static `.glassEffect(.regular, in: Capsule())` plus a shadow. Build 2-3 distinct visual polish variants behind an in-code switch so a human can compare them on the simulator and pick one.

## Do
In Sources/DesignSystem/LiquidGlassTabBar.swift add a private enum of polish variants and a single hardcoded `private let tabBarGlassVariant` constant selecting which renders, so switching variant is a one-line edit. Implement at least two, ideally three, distinct variants applied to the bar's `.glassEffect`/selected-tab styling: (1) interactive glass via `.glassEffect(.regular.interactive(), in: Capsule())`; (2) tint the SELECTED tab using the existing `@Environment(\.accentTheme) accentTheme` (e.g. a tinted glass pill or accent-tinted background behind the selected tab) while inactive tabs stay `EnoughColor.inactiveTab`; (3) an animated glass selection that moves/morphs when `selection` changes (e.g. wrap the tabs in a `GlassEffectContainer` and animate the selection indicator). Keep the tap-target fix from convert-tabbar-a (the `.contentShape(Rectangle())`) intact in every variant, keep the four `EnoughTab` cases and their symbols/titles/axIDs unchanged, and keep the `#Preview`. Consult the axiom-swiftui and axiom-design skills and the liquid-glass-auditor guidance for correct iOS 26 Liquid Glass usage.

## Done when
The app builds and runs; the bottom tab bar renders one of the polish variants; changing the single `tabBarGlassVariant` constant to each of its values and re-running shows a visibly distinct Liquid Glass treatment for each, all four tabs remain visible and correctly labelled, and every tab is still fully tappable across its whole cell (the convert-tabbar-a fix is preserved). No variant regresses tap responsiveness or hides a tab.
