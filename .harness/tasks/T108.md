## Overview
The custom bottom tab bar in Sources/DesignSystem/LiquidGlassTabBar.swift only registers taps that land almost exactly on a tab's glyph/text; taps in the surrounding empty area of a tab cell do nothing. Make each tab's whole cell tappable.

## Do
In Sources/DesignSystem/LiquidGlassTabBar.swift, inside the per-tab Button label (the VStack with the Image + Text, around lines 15-26), add `.contentShape(Rectangle())` AFTER the two `.frame(...)` modifiers (`.frame(maxWidth: .infinity)` and `.frame(minHeight: 58)`) so the entire expanded frame becomes the hit-test area rather than just the opaque glyph/text. The button keeps `.buttonStyle(PlainButtonStyle())`. Do NOT change the bar's appearance, layout, spacing, colours, glass effect, or shadow — this is a hit-testing fix only.

## Done when
Tapping anywhere within a tab's full cell (including the empty space above/below/beside the glyph, out to the edges of that tab's equal-width column) reliably switches selection to that tab; verified on the simulator by tapping near the top, bottom, and side edges of each of the four tabs (Learn, Reviews, Progress, Browse) and confirming each selection changes. The bar looks visually identical to before (no layout, spacing, colour, or glass change).
